About Me

Vaccination has been one of the greatest medical breakthrough in saving countless lives. It is also known as immunization, or commonly 'shots'. Eradicating small-pox completely and controlling polio through global vaccination has been one of the few success stories in public health. Vaccines are generally made from the the very germ that causes the disease. Vaccines come in many forms: live-weakened (attenuated), killed, inactivated toxins, proteins etc. Administering vaccines in small doses, early in life, teaches our immune system to identify and protect against that germ through one's life. In this project, we have attempted to reinforce our data analytics skills to acquire, analyze and visualize datasets from UNICEF, WHO and World Bank. We further aim to objectively understand and appreciate the role of global vaccination in public health using web visualization techniques. Details of the project can be found in Project Proposal
